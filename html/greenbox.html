Greenbox

TLDR

In a university course teams of to-be designers and UX researchers (including me) helped a startup to improve their gardening app with usability testing and redesign.

Origin

Grüner Daumen is an upcoming gardening app that is designed to inspire and support users with their plant care. Greenbox had questions regarding the usability and design of their application:

  • Is the workflow in agreement with the users' expectations?
  • Does the app facilitate inutitive and easy use?
  • Do users perceive the app as playful and yet legitimate?

1st usability test

In a first usability test participants were introduced to the app with a scenario and asked to perform several tasks while thinking aloud. The participant's interactions with the application was recorded with a camera and evaluated later. Two questionnaires (QUESI, meCUE) were employed to assess the intuitiveness and the overall impression of the product. Additionally, a heuristic analysis (following Nielsen) was performed by the UX research team. The insights genereated from these methods were condensed and forwarded to the design team.

Redesign

The design team used the collected insights to produce a redesigned version of the app.

2nd usability test

In a second usability test the redesigned version was evaluated and the first version compared with the redesigned version. The same methods were used as in the first usability test to allow for better comparison. Users experienced less problems with the redesigned version and rated it also higher in all categories. Further ideas for improvement could be identified during the second test.

Reflection

During this project I learnt a lot about the design, conduction and evaluation of usability tests. Experiencing that the insights generated through our group's work were of great use for the startup was a big motivational factor. The cooperation and communication between the design team, the UX team and the startup was challenging, but rewarded us with different perspectives and insights in the process.
